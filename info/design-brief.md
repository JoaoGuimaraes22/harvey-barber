# Design Brief — Harvey Cabeleireiro

## Niche
Men's Barbershop / Cabeleireiro Masculino — Carcavelos, Portugal

## Mood & Vibe
Vintage-classic meets bold and masculine. Dark, moody base with warm gold accents that nod to the shop's car movie memorabilia and decades of history. The feel should be established and confident — not a trendy hipster barbershop, but an institution. Strong typography inspired by old-school barbershop signage and cinema posters, with subtle textures (grain, leather-like) to avoid a flat/sterile look. Modern enough to feel professional, with a warm vintage soul underneath.

## Colour Direction
- **Primary**: Orange-gold (from logo) — confirmed brand colour, aged brass/cinema gold feel
- **Secondary**: Deep burgundy or leather brown (#6B1D1D / #4A2C2A range)
- **Background**: Dark charcoal/near-black (#1A1A1A)
- **Text**: Cream/off-white (#F5F0E8) — softer than pure white, more vintage
- **Accents**: Gold used sparingly for CTAs, borders, highlights

## References
None provided — work from the established mood direction.

## Hero Section
Responsive hero with two approaches:
- **Mobile**: Full-screen vertical video (existing shop footage shot on mobile showing the establishment and atmosphere). Overlaid text + CTA.
- **Tablet/Desktop**: Moody full-screen dark image of the shop (barber chair, interior, or memorabilia wall) with bold overlaid headline text and booking CTA.

Both versions should feel cinematic — the mobile video plays to the vertical format, desktop uses a high-impact still with strong typography.

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize
1. **20+ years of loyalty** — show through testimonials highlighting decades-long customers, a timeline or heritage element, trust indicators
2. **The team's skill** — feature David "the artist" and the team, close-up quality shots, before/after potential
3. **Warm, personal atmosphere** — convey through warm tones, real photos (not stock), friendly copy, welcoming language
4. **Affordable quality** — subtle pricing visibility, value messaging without looking cheap

## Things to Avoid
- Stock photos or generic barbershop imagery
- Hipster barbershop clichés (crossed razors, "est." badges)
- Bright/neon colours
- Overly corporate or sterile feel
- Flat, textureless design

## Section Notes
- **Reviews/Testimonials**: Lean into the long-term loyalty angle — highlight reviews that mention 10, 15, 20+ years of patronage. Use warm gold accent for star ratings.
- **About**: Tell the story of Harvey and the team. The car movie memorabilia is a unique detail worth featuring visually.
- **Services**: Keep simple and clear. Affordable pricing is a selling point — don't hide it.
- **Contact/Location**: Emphasize appointment-based booking (phone call CTA). Include Google Maps embed. Show hours prominently.
- **Gallery**: Real photos of the shop, the team at work, the memorabilia. Warm colour grading to match the vintage mood.
